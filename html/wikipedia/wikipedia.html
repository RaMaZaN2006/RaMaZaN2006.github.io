Overview

A nail is a horn-like keratinous envelope covering the tips of the fingers and toes in humans and other primates. Nails are similar to claws in other animals. Fingernails and toenails are made of a tough protective protein called keratin.skin appendages in the form of dense horny plates (homologue of claws[1]) on the dorsal surface of the ends of the fingers of the upper and lower extremities of humans and most primates. Nails are derivatives of the epidermis. Fingernails and toenails are made of a strong, protective protein called alpha keratin, which is a polymer. Alpha-keratin is found in the hooves, claws and horns of vertebrates[2].

Anatomy

The nail consists of several parts, including the nail plate, nail bed, cuticle, and nail matrix. The nail plate is the visible part of the nail. The cuticle is the tissue that covers the base of the nail. The nail matrix is the tissue that produces new nail cells.The nail consists of the nail plate, the nail matrix and the nail bed underneath, as well as the folds surrounding it[4]. The anterior edge of the nail plate is free, the posterior and lateral edges are surrounded by a skin fold. The upper part of the skin fold moves onto the nail plate and is called the proximal ridge (eponychium [5]), which plays a protective role, preventing foreign bodies and bacteria from penetrating the growth zone of the nail. The edge of the cushion represents keratinized cells. Drying and then peeling off, this edge gives rise to hangnails.

Nail Care

Maintaining healthy nails involves keeping them clean, trimmed, and moisturized. Regular nail trimming, avoiding nail biting, and protecting nails from trauma can help keep nails in good condition.There is a distinction between the body and the root of the nail. The nail root is the back of the nail plate below the proximal nail fold. Only a small part of the nail root protrudes from under the nail fold in the form of a whitish crescent-shaped area (nail socket). The nail root lies at the back of the nail bed and is called the matrix. The matrix is ​​the place of formation of the nail plate; it consists of epithelial cells. In the spinous layer of these cells there are onychoblasts - cells that form the nail, which turn into the horny plates of the nail.The basis of the nail plate is keratin, a protein also present in the skin. Hair is also formed from it. The density of keratin in nails and hair is due to the fact that this protein contains a significant number of sulfur atoms. The bonds formed between molecules strengthen the protein, making it solid. In many ways, the amount of sulfur (or more precisely, cysteine, an amino acid that includes sulfur) is determined not only by its content in the body, but also by hereditary characteristics.

History

The history of nail care dates back thousands of years to ancient civilizations. Egyptians and Babylonians are known to have used various techniques and materials for nail care and decoration. Nail care has evolved over time with the development of modern nail care products and techniques.Nails grow slower than hair. On average, fingernails grow by 1-2 mm per week, and toenails grow by 0.25-1 mm. Complete nail renewal occurs on average once every six months. Hormonal changes (adolescence, the period before menstruation, pregnancy, in spring) can affect the rate of nail growth, as well as activities that lead to nail wear (typing, habit of constantly biting nails, hand massage, etc.). An overly strict diet (low in fat, protein, vitamins) and diseases accompanied by impaired blood circulation and metabolism slow down the growth of nails. The normal nail plate (flat, smooth, pink, slightly shiny) has a thickness of 0.3 mm.
